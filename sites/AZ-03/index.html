What's in the app

4 Pages, Each One
Does Something Different.

We split NaviCare into four main pages so it stays organized and easy to navigate. Here's what each one does.

This is the main page. It shows health news and articles pulled from places like the CDC and WHO. There's a slideshow at the top with some of the latest articles and info cards at the bottom you can click on as well.

This is where you can see your upcoming doctor appointments. Each one shows the date, time, location, and if you click "more details" it gives you extra stuff like what to bring or how to prepare.

This page has a chat-style assistant that asks you questions and helps you determine whether or not you should see a doctor, and can help you book an appointment. It's a prototype and is not a sustitute for real medical advice — we made sure to put a disclaimer regarding that that.

This is the Profile page. Here you can see and edit your personal info like your name, birthday, address, phone number, insurance, doctor's name, and billing info. You click Edit to change those areas and Save when you're done.

About the App

Why Did We Make This?

We felt like a lot of health apps are either too complicated or too expensive to access. We wanted to make something that felt simple and clean — kind of like if MyChart got a redesign that normal people could actually use without getting confused.

We're three sophomores at the Phoenix Coding Academy in Phoenix, Arizona. Our school is really focused on coding and tech, so this was an important project for us. It took us several weeks to get it all done, and we split the workload into tasks specific to each role.
